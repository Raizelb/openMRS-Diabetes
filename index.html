openMRS-Diabetes

Brought to you by Team 13

Abstract

As of 2014, an estimated 387 million people have diabetes worldwide, with type 2 DM making up about 90% of the cases. This represents 8.3% of the adult population, with equal rates in both women and men. Being able to monitor oneâ€™s body weight, physical exercise, whole grains and fibre diet and smoking behaviour may help reduce diabetes as well as improve the condition of daily patients.

Healthcare system is improving from a doctor-centric world to a patient-centric one based on personal healthcare record and open-source software. This project aims to enable users to use mobile applications that will track their vital signs via wearable devices (iOS HealthKit, Android GoogleFit or Microsoft Health), keep track of their lifestyle as well as medical/family record. This data will then be sent to an OpenMRS (Open Medical Record System) that acts like a concept dictionary based on the principle that information should be stored in a way which makes it easy to summarize and analyse. Furthermore, the data will then be processed and analysed being accessible via a mobile application aiming to prevent possible causes of diabetes and making an impact in quality of care, safety and efficiency.
